Life of Lord Krishna

Lord Krishna was born in a prison cell in Mathura, ending King Kansa’s tyranny. He spent his childhood in Gokul, performing many divine acts. From lifting Govardhan Hill to playful leelas with Radha and gopis, Krishna's life is a symbol of divinity, joy, and justice.

Krishna's Teachings

  • Do your duty without attachment to the results.
  • Control your mind and desires.
  • Live with love, truth, and compassion.
  • Always stand up for righteousness (Dharma).

Bhagavad Gita Wisdom

"You have the right to work, but not to the fruits of work."

Krishna’s conversation with Arjuna on the battlefield of Kurukshetra forms the Bhagavad Gita. It teaches spiritual wisdom, duty, self-control, and the importance of devotion and surrender to God.

Advice to Students

  • Focus on learning, not just marks.
  • Have faith in yourself and God.
  • Develop discipline, courage, and a positive attitude.
  • Serve others selflessly and respect elders.

Divine Pastimes

From stealing butter, dancing with the gopis, to showing the universe in his mouth—Krishna’s leelas are full of joy, mischief, and divinity. These pastimes inspire devotion and celebrate his divine nature.

Attributes of Krishna

  • Compassionate and loving
  • Playful yet wise
  • Protector of Dharma
  • Charioteer, guide, and God
